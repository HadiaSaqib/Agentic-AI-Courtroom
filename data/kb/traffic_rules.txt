Speed limits:
  - No person shall drive exceeding maximum speed fixed by or under the Ordinance or other law.
  - Government may fix area/road-specific max speeds and erect signs accordingly.
Weight & tyres:
  - Vehicles must use pneumatic tyres unless prescribed otherwise.
  - Vehicle unladen, laden, and axle weights must not exceed values in registration certificate.
Weighment:
  - Authorized officer may require conveyance to weighing device; vehicle may be detained until compliance.
Restrictions on use:
  - Government may prohibit/restrict classes of vehicles on roads/bridges by notification; signs to be erected.
Traffic signs:
  - Government may erect traffic signs per Ninth Schedule; signs must conform to size, colour, and meaning.
Parking and halting:
  - Government may determine parking places, stands, and stopping points; public service vehicle stopping rules may differ.
Main roads:
  - Certain roads designated as main roads with driving regulations (Tenth Schedule).
Obeying signs & signals:
  - Drivers must obey mandatory traffic signs, Tenth Schedule driving regulations, traffic signals, and police directions.
Signals:
  - Drivers must make signals as per Eleventh Schedule when required; mechanical/electrical devices may be used.
Left-hand control vehicles:
  - Left-hand control vehicles must be equipped with prescribed signalling device in working order.
Leaving vehicle in dangerous position:
  - Vehicles must not be left in positions likely to cause danger or obstruction.
Riding on running boards:
  - Carrying persons on running boards prohibited (exceptions for armed forces in specific notifications).
Obstruction of driver & stationary vehicles:
  - Items or persons must not be placed to hamper driver; stationary vehicles must have driver present or brakes applied.
Pillion & helmet:
  - No more than one pillion passenger on two-wheelers; rider and pillion must wear crash helmet meeting requirements.
Document production:
  - Drivers must produce licence and registration certificate on demand; transport vehicles must also produce fitness certificate and permits.
Railway crossings:
  - Transport vehicles must stop before certain level crossings and employ a person to walk before the vehicle where required.
Duty to stop:
  - Driver must stop when required by police, animal handler, or when involved in accident; provide name/address and owner info.
Accident duties:
  - Provide medical help, report injuries/damage, furnish information to police/transport officers, and present vehicle for inspection if required.
Inspection & removal:
  - Authorized persons may inspect vehicles involved in accidents and remove them for examination (vehicle to be returned within 48 hours).
Rule-making powers:
  - Government may make detailed rules for signalling devices, removal/custody of broken/abandoned vehicles, weighing devices, exemptions, parking management, and related safety measures.
